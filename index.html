About.

The Background

The naming system of the Akan people is as distinct as that which elders in Togo (the Ewe people), the Ga people, Benin (the Fon people), and the African diaspora pass on to toddlers.

System

The Akan people use the Kwa language group’s system of timekeeping, which is based on a six-day week (‘nnanson‘), with the seventh day inclusive.New born babys are given this names after their birth days.

Variation

Aside the main day names, there are variants and flexibly diverse adjustments.

Standard

We decided to use the famous and most unbiased naming variation to ensure simple memorization
